---
title: Wire an H-bridge
description: Drive a DC motor or 4-wire stepper safely using an L293D, L298N, or similar.
---

An Arduino's pins can only push tens of milliamps. Motors want hundreds, sometimes thousands. An **H-bridge** is the chip in between — a switching network that takes small logic-level signals from the Arduino and uses them to flip much larger currents from a separate power supply, in either direction.

This guide covers two cases:

- A DC motor (one or two coils, you control speed and direction).
- A 4-wire bipolar stepper motor (used by the [Stepper](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/stepper) node).

## Parts

- An H-bridge module: L293D, L298N, or equivalent.
- A DC motor *or* a 4-wire bipolar stepper motor.
- An external power supply rated for your motor (commonly 6V, 9V, or 12V).
- Jumper wires.

## Shared rule: ground

<Callout type="warn" title="Connect grounds together">
The Arduino's GND, the H-bridge's GND, and the external power supply's negative terminal must all share a connection. Without a common ground, the logic signals between the Arduino and the H-bridge will not work.
</Callout>

Without this you typically see: motor twitches occasionally, runs only at full speed regardless of input, or the Arduino resets at random.

## DC motor

| Connection | From | To |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Logic power | Arduino 5V | H-bridge logic VCC |
| Ground | Arduino GND | H-bridge GND *and* power supply ground |
| Motor power | External supply (+) | H-bridge motor VS / VMot |
| Speed | Arduino PWM pin | H-bridge enable pin |
| Direction A | Arduino digital pin | H-bridge IN1 |
| Direction B | Arduino digital pin | H-bridge IN2 |
| Motor | Both motor leads | H-bridge OUT1 and OUT2 |

The combination of IN1/IN2 controls direction:

- IN1 HIGH, IN2 LOW → forward
- IN1 LOW, IN2 HIGH → reverse
- Both LOW → coast (motor spins down freely)
- Both HIGH → brake (motor coil is shorted, decelerates fast)

In Microflow you control these pins with [LED](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/led) or [Relay](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/relay) nodes; PWM speed control uses the LED node on the enable pin (LED brightness = motor speed).

## 4-wire bipolar stepper

For steppers, use the [Stepper](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/stepper) node in **4-wire (H-bridge)** mode.

| Connection | From | To |
| --- | --- | --- |
| IN1 | H-bridge IN1 | An Arduino digital pin |
| IN2 | H-bridge IN2 | An Arduino digital pin |
| IN3 | H-bridge IN3 | An Arduino digital pin |
| IN4 | H-bridge IN4 | An Arduino digital pin |
| GND | H-bridge GND | Arduino GND *and* motor power supply GND |
| VS / VMot | H-bridge motor power | External power supply |
| Coil A | Motor coil A (two wires) | H-bridge OUT1 + OUT2 |
| Coil B | Motor coil B (two wires) | H-bridge OUT3 + OUT4 |

<Callout type="info" title="Which wires belong to which coil?">
A 4-wire stepper has two coils. Wires on the same coil will read low resistance (a few ohms) on a multimeter. Wires from different coils will read open circuit.
</Callout>

## Verify it works

Add the relevant Express node ([LED](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/led), [Relay](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/relay), or [Stepper](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/stepper)) to your flow with the matching pin numbers, then drive it from a [Constant](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/generate/constant), [Interval](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/generate/interval), or [Trigger](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/generate/trigger).

If the motor sits silent: check the shared ground. If the motor whines but does not turn (steppers): coil pairing is probably swapped — try reversing IN3 and IN4 in your wiring.
