---
title: Collaborate on a flow without hardware
description: Edit and reason about a flow with someone who does not have a board on their desk.
---

Only one collaborator can run the flow against a real Arduino at a time — there is just one physical board. The other editors see the canvas but no live values. This guide shows how to work productively from the "no hardware" side.

For *why* this limitation exists, see [Concepts: collaboration](/docs/concepts/collaboration#one-flow-multiple-machines-one-board).

## Substitute the world with Constants

Replace each Sense node temporarily with a [Constant](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/generate/constant) producing a representative value. A potentiometer becomes a Constant at `512`. A button becomes a Constant toggled by a [Trigger](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/generate/trigger).

This lets you exercise the downstream Shape/Decide/Express logic end-to-end. The Express side does nothing physical without a board — but it runs, and its inputs are visible.

## Observe with Monitor

Drop [Monitor](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/internal/monitor) nodes liberally. They are the eyes of a hardware-less collaborator: each one shows the live value passing a particular point on the canvas.

Pair Monitors with the Constants from the previous step. You can change a Constant, watch the value propagate, and confirm the flow behaves the way you expect — all without touching a wire.

## Coordinate with the cable-holder

The collaborator who *does* have the board plugged in is the one who can verify "it works in the real world." A useful split:

- **Hardware-less collaborator** — builds and tweaks the flow, places Monitors, names nodes, writes Notes.
- **Cable-holder** — switches the Constants back to Sense nodes when ready, watches the LED actually light up, reports back.

Switching a Constant back to a Sense node is fast — drag the new node in, rewire the edge, delete the Constant.

## Use Notes to leave context

A [Note](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/internal/note) node is free-text on the canvas. Use it to flag:

- "Constant here — replace with potentiometer on A0 before running."
- "Threshold value picked from yesterday's sensor test; verify."
- "TODO: wire H-bridge."

Notes travel with the flow file and are visible to every collaborator.
