---
title: Flash firmware manually
description: Re-upload StandardFirmata to a board that is misbehaving or running someone else's sketch.
---

Microflow Studio uploads StandardFirmata automatically the first time you connect a board. Sometimes you need to do it again — for example after a different program (the Arduino IDE, another tool, or another user's sketch) has overwritten what was on the board.

## When to re-flash

- Studio detects the board but never reaches **Running**.
- The board reaches **Running** but pins do not respond to your flow.
- You used the board with the Arduino IDE and then came back to Microflow.
- A new firmware version of Microflow ships with an updated StandardFirmata.

## Steps

1. Make sure the board is plugged in and detected by your operating system. If not, see [Install board drivers](/docs/how-to/install-board-drivers).
2. In Microflow Studio, open the connection panel at the bottom of the canvas.
3. Click the menu next to the connection state and choose **Re-flash firmware** (label may also say *Upload firmware* depending on version).
4. Studio reports **Uploading**. Wait 30–60 seconds — do not unplug the board.
5. The state advances to **Running**. The board is fresh.

## Verify it worked

Drop a [Monitor](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/internal/monitor) node onto the canvas, connect any sense node to it, and watch values appear. If the Monitor stays blank, see [Pin conflicts](/docs/troubleshooting/pin-conflicts) or [Board not detected](/docs/troubleshooting/board-not-detected).

## If re-flashing keeps failing

The most common cause is **another program holding the serial port**. The Arduino IDE's Serial Monitor is the usual suspect. Close any other program that talks to the board and try again.

If the failure persists across reboots, the board may have a bootloader issue. Use the Arduino IDE to upload the *Bare Minimum* example sketch first — if even that fails, the board needs hardware-level recovery, which is outside Microflow's scope.
