A potentiometer is a dial or slider that outputs a value proportional to its position. Think of a volume knob — all the way down gives you a low number, all the way up gives you a high number.

Use it to give yourself real-time manual control over any value in your flow.

**Example uses:**
- Control LED brightness by turning a knob
- Set motor speed with a slider
- Adjust the pitch of a piezo buzzer
- Scrub through LED matrix animation frames

## Output values

The potentiometer outputs a number between **0 and 1023**:
- **0** = one extreme (fully turned or slid one way)
- **1023** = the other extreme (fully turned or slid the other way)

Because this range is large, you will often pair the potentiometer with a [Map](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/shape/map) node to scale the value to something more useful — for example, 0–180 for a servo position, or 0–255 for LED brightness.

## Settings

Open the settings panel to configure:

- **Pin** — which analog pin on your Arduino the potentiometer is connected to (analog pins are labelled A0, A1, A2, etc.)

## 2-channel sliding potentiometer

Some linear (sliding) potentiometers have two independent channels — two separate resistive tracks built into one physical slider. This lets you control two values independently with a single component.
