---
title: Oscillator
description: Create a repeating wave-like pattern of numbers that goes up and down.
---

An Oscillator generates a continuously repeating pattern of numbers that rises and falls in a wave. Where a sensor only fires when something in the physical world changes, an Oscillator produces its own changing values driven purely by time.

**Wave types:**
- **Sine wave** — a smooth, curved wave (like ocean waves). Good for slow, natural-feeling transitions
- **Square wave** — a sharp on/off pattern that toggles between two values. Good for blinking or pulsing at a fixed rate
- **Triangle wave** — a straight-line up and down pattern. Similar to sine but with sharper turning points
- **Sawtooth wave** — a gradual rise followed by a sudden drop. Good for one-directional sweeps that reset

**Examples:**
- Create a pulsing LED that smoothly fades in and out using a sine wave
- Blink an LED at a precise frequency using a square wave
- Make a servo motor sweep back and forth automatically
- Drive a repeating animation in a Figma prototype

## Settings

Open the settings panel to configure:

- **Wave type** — the shape of the repeating pattern (sine, square, triangle, sawtooth)
- **Frequency** — how fast the wave repeats, in Hz (cycles per second). 1 Hz = one full cycle per second. 0.5 Hz = one cycle every two seconds. 10 Hz = ten cycles per second
- **Amplitude** — how large the swings are. An amplitude of 100 means the wave travels from −100 to +100
- **Phase** — where in the cycle the wave starts. Leave this at 0 for most uses
