A stepper motor moves in discrete steps, giving you precise control over position and speed. Unlike a servo (limited to 180° or continuous without position feedback), a stepper can rotate any number of degrees in either direction and always knows exactly where it is.

Steppers are commonly used for CNC machines, 3D printers, camera sliders, turntables, and any project that needs repeatable, precise motion.

<Callout title="Driver board required">
Stepper motors cannot be driven directly from an Arduino pin. You need a **driver board** that sits between the Arduino and the motor. The driver handles the high-current switching while the Arduino sends simple step and direction signals on two digital pins.

Common driver boards: **A4988**, **DRV8825**, **TMC2209**, **EasyDriver**, **TB6600**.
</Callout>

## Inputs

- **Value** — relative move: number of steps to move. Positive values move clockwise, negative values move counter-clockwise
- **To** — absolute move: move to a specific step position (relative to the zero point)
- **Stop** — stop the motor. If acceleration is set, the motor decelerates to a stop
- **Zero** — reset the position counter to zero without moving the motor

## Outputs

- **Position** — the current position in steps after a move completes
- **Complete** — fires an event when a move finishes

## Settings

Open the settings panel to configure:

- **Interface** — choose between "Step/Dir driver" (for A4988, DRV8825, etc.) or "4-wire (H-bridge)" (for L293D, L298N, or similar dual H-bridge boards)
- **Step pin** (driver mode) — the digital pin connected to the driver's STEP input
- **Dir pin** (driver mode) — the digital pin connected to the driver's DIR (direction) input
- **IN1–IN4** (4-wire mode) — the four digital pins connected to the H-bridge inputs controlling the two motor coils
- **Steps/rev** — steps per full revolution of your motor. Most standard stepper motors are 200 steps/rev (1.8° per step). If your driver is set to microstepping, multiply accordingly (e.g. 1/16 microstepping on a 200-step motor = 3200)
- **Speed** — maximum speed in steps per second
- **Acceleration** — acceleration and deceleration rate in steps per second squared. Set to 0 for instant speed changes (no ramping)

## Wiring

A typical setup with a step/direction driver board (A4988, DRV8825, etc.):

| Connection | From | To |
| --- | --- | --- |
| STEP | Driver board STEP input | A digital pin on your Arduino |
| DIR | Driver board DIR input | A digital pin on your Arduino |
| GND | Driver board GND | Arduino GND *and* motor power supply GND |
| VMOT | Driver board motor power | External power supply (12V, 24V, etc.) |
| Motor | Driver board motor outputs (A1, A2, B1, B2) | Stepper motor coils |

<Callout type="warn" title="Use a separate power supply for the motor">
Stepper motors draw significant current (often 1–2A per coil). Never power them from the Arduino's 5V or VIN pin. Use a dedicated power supply rated for your motor's voltage and current requirements.

Connect the power supply ground to both the driver board GND and the Arduino GND — this shared ground is required for the step/direction signals to work correctly.
</Callout>

### 4-wire H-bridge wiring (L293D, L298N)

If you are using a dual H-bridge board with IN1–IN4 inputs, select "4-wire (H-bridge)" in the interface setting.

| Connection | From | To |
| --- | --- | --- |
| IN1 | H-bridge IN1 | A digital pin on your Arduino |
| IN2 | H-bridge IN2 | A digital pin on your Arduino |
| IN3 | H-bridge IN3 | A digital pin on your Arduino |
| IN4 | H-bridge IN4 | A digital pin on your Arduino |
| GND | H-bridge GND | Arduino GND *and* motor power supply GND |
| VS / VMot | H-bridge motor power | External power supply (12V, etc.) |
| Motor A | H-bridge OUT1 + OUT2 | Stepper motor coil A |
| Motor B | H-bridge OUT3 + OUT4 | Stepper motor coil B |

<Callout type="info" title="Which wires are which coil?">
A 4-wire stepper motor has two coils (A and B), each with two wires. If you are unsure which wires belong to which coil, use a multimeter in resistance mode. Wires from the same coil will show low resistance (a few ohms). Wires from different coils will show no continuity.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Microstepping">
Most driver boards support microstepping (1/2, 1/4, 1/8, 1/16, 1/32 steps) configured via jumpers or solder bridges on the driver board itself. Microstepping gives smoother motion and finer positioning but reduces torque and maximum speed. Adjust the **steps/rev** setting to match your microstepping configuration.
</Callout>

## Firmware requirement

The stepper node uses Firmata's AccelStepper protocol. Your Arduino must be flashed with **ConfigurableFirmata** with the AccelStepperFirmata feature enabled. StandardFirmata does not include stepper support.
