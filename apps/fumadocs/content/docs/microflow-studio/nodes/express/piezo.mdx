A piezo is a small buzzer that can make sounds. It is not a speaker — it produces simple electronic tones rather than rich audio — but it is perfect for beeps, alerts, or simple melodies.

**Example uses:**
- Play a beep when a button is pressed
- Sound an alert when a sensor crosses a threshold
- Play a short melody when a project completes a task

## What you can do

- **Play a tone** — send a frequency value (in Hz) to make the piezo buzz at that pitch. Higher numbers = higher pitch. For example, 440 Hz is the musical note A.
- **Play a melody** — compose a sequence of notes in the node's settings and trigger them to play in order.
- **Stop** — send a signal to stop any sound currently playing.

## Settings

Open the settings panel to configure:

- **Pin** — which digital pin on your Arduino the piezo is connected to
- **Melody** — compose a custom melody by entering notes and their durations
