title: Servo

A servo motor moves to a position or spins at a speed when you send it a value. Unlike a basic motor that just spins freely, a servo gives you precise control — either over *where* it points, or *how fast* it spins.

Servos are commonly used for robot arms, mechanical linkages, automated valves, and any project that needs controlled movement.

<Callout title="Two types of servo">
**Positional (standard)** — rotates to a specific angle between 0° and 180° and holds there. Send a number between 0 and 180 to set the angle. If the packaging does not mention "360", it is almost certainly positional.

**Continuous (360)** — spins continuously at a controlled speed and direction. Send a number between 0 and 180, where 90 = stopped, below 90 = one direction, above 90 = the other. Packaging or markings will usually say "360".
</Callout>

## Inputs

- **Value** — for a positional servo: the target angle in degrees (0–180). For a continuous servo: the speed and direction (0–180, where 90 = stopped)

## Settings

Open the settings panel to configure:

- **Pin** — which PWM-capable pin on your Arduino the servo signal wire is connected to. Servos require a PWM pin
- **Type** — positional or continuous

## Wiring

A servo has three wires. The color coding below is common but not universal — verify with your servo's documentation.

| Wire color | Function | Connect to |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Brown or black | Ground | GND on Arduino *and* external power supply |
| Red | Power (5V) | External 5V power supply |
| Orange or yellow | Signal | A PWM pin on your Arduino |

<Callout type="warn" title="Use a separate power supply for the servo">
Servos can draw significantly more current than an Arduino can safely provide. Powering a servo from the Arduino's 5V pin may cause the board to reset, behave erratically, or sustain damage.

Power the servo from a **separate 5V power supply** and connect only the signal wire and ground to the Arduino. Connect the negative (GND) of the external supply to the Arduino's GND — this shared ground is required for the signal wire to work correctly.
</Callout>
