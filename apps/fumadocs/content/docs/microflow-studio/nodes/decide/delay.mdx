---
title: Delay
description: Make your flow wait before continuing.
---

The Delay node holds a signal for a set amount of time before passing it on. Instead of acting immediately when something happens, you can add a pause.

**Examples:**
- Wait 2 seconds after a button is pressed before turning on an LED
- Keep a light on for 10 seconds after motion is detected, then let it turn off
- Add a gap between steps in a sequence

## Debouncing

When **debounce** is enabled, the timer resets every time a new signal arrives. The Delay only fires after signals have stopped arriving for the full duration.

This is useful for sensors that fire many times in quick succession. Instead of acting on every single reading, you wait for activity to settle down first.

**Example:** A vibration sensor fires 20 times when tapped. With a 500 ms debounce, the Delay waits until tapping has fully stopped for half a second before passing the signal through.

## Settings

Open the settings panel to configure:

- **Duration** — how long to wait, in milliseconds (1000 ms = 1 second)
- **Debounce** — when enabled, the timer resets on every new incoming signal instead of starting once
