Microflow Studio is the desktop app at the heart of Microflow. You build projects by dragging [nodes](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes) onto a canvas and connecting them with [edges](/docs/microflow-studio/edges) — Microflow then runs the whole thing on your Arduino over USB.

No code, no compilation. Edit the canvas while it runs and the changes apply live.

[Download Microflow Studio](https://github.com/xiduzo/microflow-t-stack/releases)

## The canvas

When you open Microflow Studio, you see a large blank canvas — this is where you build your project.

- **Add a node** — right-click anywhere on the canvas, or press `n`, to open the node menu.
- **Move nodes** — click and drag a node to reposition it.
- **Connect nodes** — click and drag from a node's right-hand handle to another node's left-hand handle.
- **Pan the canvas** — click and drag on an empty area.
- **Zoom** — scroll wheel, or pinch on a trackpad.

Once your flow is ready and your board is connected, Microflow runs it automatically — there is no "run" button.

For the conceptual model behind the canvas, see [Flows, signals, and events](/docs/concepts/flows-signals-events).
