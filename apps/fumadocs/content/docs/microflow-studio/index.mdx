Microflow Studio is a desktop application that lets you build interactive hardware projects on a **visual canvas**. You connect blocks (called [nodes](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes)) together with lines (called [edges](/docs/microflow-studio/edges)), and Microflow runs it all on your Arduino automatically.

No coding needed.

[Download Microflow Studio](https://github.com/xiduzo/microflow/releases)

---

## The canvas

When you open Microflow Studio, you see a large blank canvas — this is where you build your project.

- **Add a node:** Right-click anywhere on the canvas to open the node menu and choose a building block to add
- **Move nodes:** Click and drag a node to reposition it
- **Connect nodes:** Click and drag from the green dot (right side of a node) to the blue dot (left side of another node) — this creates a connection
- **Pan the canvas:** Click and drag on an empty area of the canvas
- **Zoom:** Use your scroll wheel, or pinch on a trackpad

Once your flow is ready and your board is connected, Microflow runs it automatically — you do not need to press a "run" button.

## Connecting your microcontroller

A **microcontroller** (like an Arduino) is the small board you connect to your computer via USB. It is what physically controls your components — LEDs, buttons, sensors, and more.

Once you plug it in, Microflow Studio detects it automatically and goes through these steps:

1. **Waiting** — looking for a board to be plugged in
2. **Detecting** — identifying which board it is
3. **Uploading** — sending your flow to the board
4. **Running** — your project is live

<Callout title="First-time setup takes a moment">
The very first time you connect a board, Microflow installs **StandardFirmata** on it. This is a small program that lets your computer communicate with the board. It only happens once per board and takes about 30–60 seconds.

Do not unplug the board while this is happening.
</Callout>

### Supported boards

Microflow Studio officially supports the following Arduino boards:

- Arduino Uno
- Arduino Mega
- Arduino Leonardo
- Arduino Micro
- Arduino Nano
- Arduino Yun

[Other boards](https://johnny-five.io/platform-support/) may work but are not guaranteed to behave correctly.

## Troubleshooting connection issues

If you see `Upload failed for unknown reasons`, your board is not connecting properly. Try the following:

### 1. Test with Arduino IDE

The **Arduino IDE** is a free program that lets you test whether your board is working at all. This helps narrow down if the problem is with your board or with Microflow.

1. Close Microflow Studio
2. Download [the StandardFirmata file for Microflow](https://github.com/xiduzo/microflow/tree/7eed72541c6d6b455cf8c84a71b737400a6d7e68/packages/flasher/StandardFirmata)
3. Install [the Arduino IDE](https://www.arduino.cc/en/software)
4. Open the Arduino IDE and plug in your board
5. Go to **Tools → Port** and select your board's port
6. Go to **Tools → Board** and select your board type (e.g. "Arduino Uno")
7. Open the StandardFirmata file you downloaded
8. Click the **Upload** button (the arrow icon)
9. Re-open Microflow Studio — your board should now connect

If the upload works in Arduino IDE, your hardware is fine and the issue is likely a Microflow bug. If it fails there too, the board or cable may be the problem.

### 2. Install board drivers

Some boards — especially cheaper Arduino clones — need an extra driver before your computer can detect them.

The most common one is the **CH34x driver**. Download it [here](https://sparks.gogo.co.nz/ch340.html), and follow [this YouTube guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MM9Fj6bwHLk) for installation steps.

<Callout type="warn" title="Still stuck?">
If nothing above worked, you can help us find the bug by sharing your **log file** — a file that records everything Microflow did, which helps us diagnose the issue.

| OS | Log file location |
| --- | --- |
| Linux | `~/.config/microflow-studio/logs/main.log` |
| macOS | `~/Library/Logs/microflow-studio/main.log` |
| Windows | `%USERPROFILE%\AppData\Roaming\microflow-studio\logs\main.log` |

[Open a bug report](https://github.com/xiduzo/microflow/issues/new?assignees=&labels=&projects=&template=bug_report.md&title=) and attach the log file. Thank you!
</Callout>
