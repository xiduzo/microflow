---
title: Fade an LED with a potentiometer
description: Read an analog sensor and use its full range to control LED brightness.
---

So far values have been on or off. A potentiometer (a "pot") gives you something richer: a value that smoothly slides between two extremes as you twist the knob. In this lesson, twisting the knob makes an LED grow brighter or dimmer.

## What you need

- A standard 10kΩ rotary potentiometer (three legs)
- An LED and a 220Ω resistor
- A breadboard and jumper wires
- An Arduino and Microflow Studio

## Step 1 — Wire the potentiometer

A pot has three legs: two outer legs and a middle "wiper". The outer legs go to power and ground, the wiper reports a voltage between them.

| Connection | From | To |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Power | Arduino 5V | One outer leg of the pot |
| Ground | Arduino GND | The other outer leg of the pot |
| Signal | Middle (wiper) leg of the pot | Arduino **A0** (an analog pin) |

## Step 2 — Wire the LED

The LED needs to be on a **PWM** pin so we can dim it. On an Uno, PWM pins are marked with a `~` (3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 11).

| Connection | From | To |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Signal | Arduino pin 9 | 220Ω resistor → long leg of the LED |
| Ground | Short leg of the LED | Arduino GND |

## Step 3 — Build the flow

In a new flow, add:

1. A [**Potentiometer**](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/sense/potentiometer) node — set its pin to `A0`.
2. A [**Map**](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/shape/map) node.
3. An [**LED**](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/express/led) node — set **Pin** to `9`.
4. Connect Potentiometer → Map → LED.

The Potentiometer outputs a number from `0` to `1023`. The LED expects a brightness from `0` to `255`. The Map node converts between them.

In the Map node settings:

- **From min** = `0`
- **From max** = `1023`
- **To min** = `0`
- **To max** = `255`

## Step 4 — Try it

Twist the knob. The LED should fade smoothly between off and full brightness.

If the brightness only changes in big jumps, your wiper might be on the wrong leg — try a different middle pin. If it stays dark or stays full-bright, double-check ground and 5V are not swapped on the outer legs.

## What just happened?

Three categories of node now appear in your flow:

- **Sense** (Potentiometer) — read a real-world signal
- **Shape** (Map) — transform a value into the right range
- **Express** (LED) — produce a real-world effect

Map's job is small but constant: it turns a number from one range (`0–1023`) into another (`0–255`). You will reach for it again whenever a sensor's "natural" range does not match what the next node expects.
