---
title: Smooth a noisy sensor
description: Use shape nodes to clean up jumpy readings before they reach an output.
---

Plug a sensor into a Monitor node and you will see it: even when nothing changes, the readings flicker. This is normal — analog inputs always carry some electrical noise. In this lesson you will *see* the noise, then *remove* it.

## What you need

- The setup from the [previous lesson](/docs/learn/fade-with-potentiometer): potentiometer on `A0`, LED on pin `9`.

<Callout type="info" title="Template available">
  Studio ships the finished flow as the **Smooth a Noisy Sensor** template — open the **Templates** page and load it to compare raw and smoothed readings right away.
</Callout>

## Step 1 — See the noise

In your flow, drag a [**Monitor**](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/internal/monitor) node onto the canvas and connect the Potentiometer's output to it.

Hold the knob completely still. The Monitor should show a value that twitches by 1–3 every reading even though you are not touching the pot. That is sensor noise.

For an LED that does not matter much. For things like motor speed, audio thresholds, or data sent to other software, noise can cause real problems.

## Step 2 — Add a Smooth node

Insert a [**Smooth**](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/shape/smooth) node between the Potentiometer and the Map node.

Connect: Potentiometer → Smooth → Map → LED. Also keep the Monitor on the Potentiometer so you can compare.

In the Smooth node settings:

- **Window** = `10` — the number of recent readings to average

Add a second Monitor and connect it to the Smooth node's output. You should see the smoothed reading change much less when you hold the knob still — and lag slightly when you move the knob fast.

## Step 3 — Tune the trade-off

Smoothing is always a trade-off:

- **Bigger window** → less noise, more lag.
- **Smaller window** → more noise, more responsive.

Try `5`, `20`, `50`. Watch how the Monitor value behaves and how the LED feels when you twist the knob.

## What just happened?

You learned three things at once:

- Shape nodes do not change *what* a flow does; they change *how* values look on the way through.
- Smoothing is a recipe — there is no "right" answer, only a choice that fits your project.
- The Monitor node is your best debugging tool. Place it before and after a node to see exactly what it is doing.

If you find yourself fighting noise often, also look at:

- [Calculate](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/shape/calculate) — for math operations.
- [Function](/docs/microflow-studio/nodes/shape/function) — for custom JavaScript when nothing else fits.
