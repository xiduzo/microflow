Microflow is a set of free tools that lets you connect physical hardware — buttons, lights, sensors, motors — and make them work together, all without writing a single line of code.

You build your project visually by dragging and dropping building blocks onto a canvas and connecting them together. Microflow handles all the technical stuff under the hood.

## What you will need

To use Microflow Studio you need:

- A **computer** running Windows, macOS, or Linux
- An **Arduino board** (Uno, Mega, Leonardo, Micro, Nano, or Yun)
- A **USB cable** to connect the Arduino to your computer
- The hardware components you want to use (buttons, LEDs, sensors, etc.)

That's it. No programming experience needed.

## What can you make?

Here are some examples to give you an idea:

- Press a button and turn on an LED
- Read a light sensor and adjust the brightness of a lamp
- Detect motion and trigger a sound
- Connect a sensor to a Figma prototype and make it react to the real world

<Callout title="Not sure which one to use?">
Start with **Microflow Studio**. It works on its own and is the core of everything. The Bridge plugins are add-ons for people who also use Figma or Penpot.
